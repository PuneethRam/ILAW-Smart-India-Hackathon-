      SUITS RELATING TO CONTRACTS

S.No. Description of suits Period of limitation Time from which period begins to run
1. For a seaman’s wages. Three years The end of the voyage during which the wages are earned.
2. For wages in the case of any other person Three years When the wages accrue due.
3. For the price of food or drink sold by the keeper of a hotel, tavern or lodging house. Three years When the food or drink is delivered.
4. For the price of lodging. Three years When the price be­comes payable.
5. Against a carrier for compensa­tion for losing or injuring goods Three years When the loss or in­jury occurs.
6. Against a carrier for compensa­tion for non-delivery of, or delay in delivering, goods. Three years When the goods ought to be delivered.
7. For the hire of animals, vehi­cles, boats or household furni­ture. Three years When the hire be­comes payable.
8. For the balance of money ad­vanced in payment of goods to be delivered. Three years When the goods ought to be delivered.
9. For the price of goods sold and delivered where no fixed period of credit is agreed upon. Three years The date of the deliv­ery of the goods.
10. For the price of goods sold and delivered to be paid for after the expiry of a fixed period of credit. Three years When the period of credit expires.
11. For the price of goods sold and delivered to be paid for by a bill of exchange, no such bill being given. Three years When the period of the proposed bill elapses.
12. For the price of trees or grow­ing crops sold by the plaintiff to the defendant where no fixed period of credit is agreed upon. Three years The date of the sale.
13. For the price of work done by the plaintiff for the defendant at his request, where no time has been fixed for payment. Three years When the work is done.
14. For money payable for money lent. Three years When the loan is made
15. For money lent under an agree­ment that it shall be payable on demand. Three years When the loan is made.
16. Like suit when the lender has given a cheque for the money. Three years When the cheque is paid.
17. For money deposited under an agreement that it shall be payable on demand, including money of a customer in the hands of his banker so payable. Three years When the demand is made.
18. For money payable to the plain­tiff for money paid for the defendant. Three years When the money is paid
19. For money payable by the de­fendant to the plaintiff for money received by the defen­dant, for the plaintiff’s use. Three years When the money is re­ceived.
20. For money payable for interest upon money due from the defendant to the plaintiff. Three years When the interest be­comes due.
